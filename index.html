Philosophy

What is Radical Simplexity?

Simplexity is the clarity that emerges only after genuinely understanding complexity — not simplification that loses depth, but the insight that becomes visible once you see the structure beneath.

Radical means a breakthrough idea that actually works. Not just different — tested, refined, and proven in practice. When the conventional approach is not reaching a student, we find the one that does.

Radical Simplexity is the drive and ambition to making mathematics clear, visual, and transferable — by going deeper, not by cutting corners — and to understanding the emergent clarity, behaviours, and patterns of mathematics.

In practice: helping students move from following steps to understanding why those steps work, so they can reason through problems they have never seen before.

Structure over steps — see the logic behind the method
Transfer, not memory — apply concepts to questions you've never seen
Beyond the exam — Mathematics as the thinking tool of life, and the learner's superpower for life

The Method

The Radical Simplexity Method

A structured, scaffolded teaching process — building from foundations to independent mathematical thinking

Scaffolded learning: Every lesson moves from shallow to deep — piece by piece, building each new idea on what came before. No step is skipped. No concept is rushed. The goal is always genuine understanding, not surface coverage.

1
Foundation

Diagnose the Gap

I begin every engagement with targeted diagnostic questions — not to test, but to locate. Where exactly does the understanding break down? A student who can solve a standard question may still have a hidden conceptual gap that causes them to freeze on unfamiliar problems. We find that gap first.

2
Concept

Rebuild the Concept

I explain the why behind the method — using diagrams, analogies, and intuitive explanations before introducing the formal approach. We scaffold from the simplest case upward: start with what the student already knows, add one new layer at a time, and confirm understanding before moving on. Nothing is skipped.

3
Application

Apply to Exam Questions

Students practise on standard questions first — to confirm the concept is solid — then on unfamiliar, multi-step problems that require transferring the idea to new contexts. I write custom questions targeted at the specific gap we identified in Step 1. Every question has a purpose.

4
Extension

Connect to the Real World

Where meaningful, I show how the mathematical concept appears in the real world — drawn from my background as an industry statistician and operations research expert in engineering, manufacturing, supply chain, and finance. This is not decoration. It deepens understanding by showing why the structure exists.

5
Mastery

Build Independence

The goal is not dependence on a tutor. The goal is a student who can think through a problem they have never seen before — confidently and systematically. We work towards this from the very first session. When a student no longer needs me, that is the best outcome.
